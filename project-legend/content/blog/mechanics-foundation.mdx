# Mechanics: The Foundation of Physics

Mechanics is often the first topic students encounter in physics. It's no coincidence—nearly every other branch of physics builds on the principles of motion, forces, and energy.

## Newton's Laws

The three laws of motion provide a framework for understanding how objects behave under the influence of forces. The key is not memorizing them, but *understanding* what each law implies and when it applies.

## Building Intuition

Before diving into problem-solving, spend time building intuition. What does it feel like when a bus accelerates? Why does a ball follow a parabolic path when thrown? These everyday experiences connect to the mathematics we use.

## Moving Forward

Once mechanics is solid, thermodynamics, waves, and electromagnetism become much easier to grasp. Take your time with the foundations.
